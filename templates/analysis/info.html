How does this work?

We would expect a completely neutral player in an unbiased environment to have a winrate of exactly the same winrate, whether the last games were won or lost. But winning or loosing a game causes the players to gain or loose MMR, therefor winning a game can decrease your chance of winning and loosing a game can increase your chance of winning. To account for that we compare you to others players. If you win more often after winning and you loose more often after loosing compared to the average player, that can be taken as an indication that you tilt.

How accurate is the score?

The score is fun statistic that can mean something, but it should not be taken as very accurate. We don't analyze enough games to safely rule out chance. There can also be other factors that influence your winrate after winning/loosing. Maybe you are more likely to get your main role or your main champion at a lower MMR. Maybe you take a long break after loosing so your mood does not influence the next game. Maybe your regular duo-partner tilts ;) Also there is a bias in the players we compare you to. The players that are entered into this website might not represent the average player at all. The real "tilters" might never enter their names in here, or people might enter a lot of "tilters" in here to confirm there suspicions.

How does the math work?

Take the winrate after winning one game minus the winrate after loosing one game. Out of the resulting scores we compute the Percentile rank. So if you get a score of 100, you have the highest score out of all players in our database. If you get a score of 50, then 50% of players in our database have a lower score than you.

Which game types are used?

At the moment we are only taking your solo ranked games into account.
